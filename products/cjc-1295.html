Growth Hormone Releasing Hormone Analogue

A modified GHRH peptide designed to extend growth hormone release — the steady-state approach to GH signaling.

CJC-1295 is a synthetic analogue of GHRH (growth hormone releasing hormone) that’s been modified at four amino acid positions to resist enzymatic breakdown. The result is a compound that extends the duration of GH release rather than creating a single sharp spike. This sustained-release profile is what distinguishes it from short-acting GHRH peptides and GH secretagogues like ipamorelin.

This version — CJC-1295 without DAC (no Drug Affinity Complex) — provides a cleaner, shorter-duration pulse compared to the DAC version. It’s the most commonly used form when the goal is to study pulsatile GH patterns that more closely mimic natural release, rather than keeping GH elevated continuously. It’s frequently paired with Ipamorelin for combined GHRH + secretagogue protocols.

Why this one matters: Ipamorelin tells the pituitary to release GH. CJC-1295 amplifies and extends that release. They work through different mechanisms — one is a secretagogue, the other is a GHRH analogue. Together, they produce a stronger, more sustained GH pulse than either one alone.
